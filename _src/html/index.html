Four Design Sprints

The team led a series of four design sprints. The first, the vision sprint, focused on getting the team and organization to align on a shared vision that would serve as a North Star for the next eight years. Design sprints two through four dialed in on three key product worksteams that were key to delivering on our CX goal: Item Design, Test Construction, and Data & Tools. While the intent of each sprint was slightly different, they all followed a similar structure.

Understanding the education ecosystem

We began each sprint by learning about our end users: students, teachers and education systems. We focused on student interviews to ensure we were always orienting work with students at the center of the story.

{% include "_components/figure.html" with { "picture": { "altText": "2 images of stickynotes on white boards mapping a process", "srcPath": "https://vmlyr-projects.s3.us-east-2.amazonaws.com/nwea-wppawards/images/four-design-mapping-ecosystem.png", "placeholderSize": "524 391", "class": "" } } %}

Mapping the ecosystem and defining intent

The team began mapping the school experience by exploring the impact of instruction, learning materials, curriculum, and how it impacts the student/teacher experience. We then looked for themes that connected various ideas. We used “How might we” framing to express how we could deliver on our intent.

The Results

Our process and product transformation helped NWEA produce prototypes based on a shared vision and user input. The design sprints allowed the team to deliver prototypes in half the time it takes their other products.
